01

Sentence in Context — Airport

Apa kamu lakukan di bandara?

Your tutor opened with this full sentence as a real-world example. It breaks down as:

Apa (what) + kamu (you) + lakukan (did/do — from laku, to do/act) + di bandara (at the airport)

Apa kamu lakukan di bandara? a-pa · ka-mu · la-ku-kan · di · ban-da-ra? What did you do at the airport?
Saya jemput keluarga Emma di bandara sa-ya · jem-put · ke-lu-ar-ga · em-ma · di · ban-da-ra I picked up Emma's family at the airport

Jemput = to pick up / collect someone. Used specifically for collecting a person from a location — airport, school, station. Not used for objects.

Lakukan
Lakukan is the verb "to do" with the -kan suffix, which makes a verb transitive (it takes an object). You will also see melakukan (the formal me- prefix version). In casual speech, lakukan works on its own. Think of apa kamu lakukan as the Indonesian equivalent of "what did you do" — a very natural, frequently used structure.

The number pattern

11–19: number + belas (except 11 = sebelas). So 12 = dua belas, 17 = tujuh belas — completely regular.

20, 30, 40…: number + puluh. So 20 = dua puluh, 30 = tiga puluh, 40 = empat puluh — again completely regular.

21, 32…: tens + ones. 21 = dua puluh satu. 35 = tiga puluh lima. No exceptions.

03

Telling the Time

Jam = hour / o'clock

Jam means both "hour" and "o'clock." To say what time it is, use jam + the number:

Jam berapa? — Jam tiga jam · be-ra-pa? — jam · ti-ga What time is it? — It's 3 o'clock
Saya bangun jam enam pagi sa-ya · ba-ngun · jam · e-nam · pa-gi I wake up at 6 in the morning

Tengah means mid / middle. Combined with time words it creates:

Jam satu
1 o'clock
jam + satu
Jam dua belas
12 o'clock / noon
jam + dua belas
Tengah malam
Midnight
tengah = mid · malam = night
Tengah hari
Midday / noon
tengah = mid · hari = day
Jam setengah tiga
Half past two (2:30)
setengah = half · tiga = 3 (half to 3)
Jam berapa?
What time is it?
The essential time question
Setengah — the half hour
Indonesian tells half-hours as "half to" the next hour — the same as Dutch. Setengah tiga = half-three = 2:30 (half an hour before 3). This trips up English speakers who expect "half past two." Remember: setengah + next hour = 30 minutes before that hour.

05

Question Words — Rule Reinforced

Question words always go first

Your tutor noted this explicitly: kapan, dimana, apa, siapa, mengapa, bagaimana all go at the very start of the sentence. The rest of the sentence stays in normal S-V-O order after the question word.

Kapan ___ ___ ka-pan · [subject] · [verb] When [subject] [verb]… → Kapan kamu berangkat? (When do you leave?)
Dimana ___ ___ di-ma-na · [subject] · [verb] Where [subject] [verb]… → Dimana kamu jemput dia? (Where do you pick her up?)

This rule makes forming questions very mechanical once you have it — swap the question word to the front, leave everything else exactly as it was in the statement.

Statement
Kamu berangkat jam tujuh
ka-mu · be-rang-kat · jam · tu-juh
You leave at 7 o'clock

Question

07

Practice Drills

Drill these until automatic
  • Count from 1 to 30 out loud without looking. Then count in reverse. Then say only the even numbers.
  • Say the time right now in Indonesian — Sekarang jam … Then say what time you woke up this morning and what time you will sleep tonight.
  • Practise setengah: what is setengah delapan? (7:30) Setengah sebelas? (10:30) Setengah tiga? (2:30). Say five half-hour times.
  • Build the airport sentence in three tenses: Saya jemput dia di bandara jam sembilan (present). Saya akan jemput dia besok (future). Saya jemput dia kemarin (past).
  • Take a statement and turn it into a kapan and dimana question: Kamu naik motor ke bandara jam tujuh → Kapan kamu naik motor ke bandara? / Dimana kamu naik motor?
  • Use naik and kemudikan correctly: naik goes with motorbikes, buses, planes (vehicles you board). Kemudikan is specifically for driving a car. Practise three sentences with each.
  • Describe your ideal day in Bali as a full sequence using jam + times: Jam enam saya bangun. Jam tujuh saya naik motor ke pantai… Use at least six time slots.

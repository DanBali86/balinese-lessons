01

Baik vs Bagus — Both Mean "Good"

Two words for good — used differently

Your tutor noted the distinction at the top of your notes with the labels attitude and quality:

Baik = good in character, morally good, kind, well-behaved. Used for people's nature, behaviour, and health.

Bagus = good in quality, nice-looking, well-made. Used for things, appearance, and quality.

Dia orang yang baik di-a · o-rang · yang · ba-ik She is a good person (good in character)
Baju kamu bagus ba-ju · ka-mu · ba-gus Your clothes are nice (good in quality/looks)
Apa kabar? — Baik, terima kasih a-pa · ka-bar? — ba-ik · te-ri-ma · ka-sih How are you? — Well, thank you (baik = doing well)
Adjective word order
Your tutor's example Baju kamu bagus shows the Indonesian adjective pattern: Noun + Possessor + Adjective. The adjective comes after the noun and its owner — the opposite of English. So "your good clothes" becomes "clothes your good" — baju kamu bagus.

04

Prepositions — Ke, Di, Dengan

Three essential prepositions

Di = in / on / at — static location. Where something is.

Ke = to — direction of movement. Where you are going.

Dengan = with — accompaniment or manner.

Saya di kantor sa-ya · di · kan-tor I am at the office (static — I am there)
Saya pergi ke kantor sa-ya · per-gi · ke · kan-tor I go to the office (movement — I am heading there)
Saya makan dengan teman sa-ya · ma-kan · de-ngan · te-man I eat with a friend
Saya pergi ke pasar dengan kamu sa-ya · per-gi · ke · pa-sar · de-ngan · ka-mu I go to the market with you
05

Time Phrases — Ini & Setelah/Sebelum

This morning / tonight / this afternoon

Your tutor showed that adding ini (this) after any time-of-day word creates "this ___":

Pagi ini  ·  Siang ini  ·  Malam ini pa-gi · i-ni  ·  si-ang · i-ni  ·  ma-lam · i-ni This morning  ·  This afternoon  ·  Tonight

This pattern works because ini means "this" and simply specifies which pagi/siang/malam you mean — today's one.

IndonesianPronunciationEnglishExample
Pagi ini pa-gi · i-ni This morning Saya bangun pagi ini — I woke up this morning
Siang ini si-ang · i-ni This afternoon Saya makan siang ini — I'm eating lunch this afternoon
Malam ini ma-lam · i-ni Tonight Saya akan makan malam ini — I will eat dinner tonight
Setelah se-te-lah After Setelah mandi, saya sarapan — After showering, I have breakfast
Sebelum se-be-lum Before Sebelum tidur, saya mandi — Before sleeping, I shower

Pom bensin vs Stasiun gas
Your tutor noted both terms for petrol station. Pom bensin is the term you will hear most commonly in Bali and everyday speech — it comes from Dutch pomp (pump) and bensin (petrol). Stasiun gas is the more formal/standard Indonesian. Use pom bensin when speaking to locals.

09

Practice Drills

Drill these until automatic
  • Describe your morning routine as a chain using setelah and sebelum: Sebelum mandi, saya bangun. Setelah mandi, saya sarapan. Setelah sarapan, saya pergi ke kantor.
  • Use both baik and bagus correctly in a sentence each. Remember: baik = character/health, bagus = quality/looks. Dia baik. Baju itu bagus.
  • Drill the ke/di distinction with every place from this lesson: Saya di pantai (I am at the beach) vs Saya pergi ke pantai (I go to the beach). Do this for kantor, pasar, restoran, rumah sakit.
  • Build "this morning / tonight / this afternoon" into three separate sentences about your actual day today.
  • Make three sentences using dengan: Saya makan dengan teman. Saya pergi ke pasar dengan kamu. Saya renang dengan dia.
  • Use tolong in three requests with places from this lesson: Tolong beli kopi di pasar. Tolong pergi ke kantor. Tolong mandi sebelum makan.
  • Say "I will buy __ at the market" with five different items, using the full sentence: Saya akan beli [item] di pasar malam ini.
